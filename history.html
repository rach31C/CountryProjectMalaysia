Malaysia's History

Predating back to the Post Classical Era, the country is filled with both rich yet dark history!

HISTORY

15th Century Malacca Trading Ports

During the Post Classical Era, many people were trading various goods within AfroEurAsia. Many merchants wanted to trade in quick and efficent methods. The best method was through maritime routes and technology. Many merchants wanted to trade with China for their silk and other goods. To do so, they went through Malacca where many merchants settled down to take breaks or start a new life.


Tugu Negara

The Tugu Negara is a national monument in Malaysia. It's a statue to commemorate the soliders who died for Malaysia's freedom, speifically against the Japanese. The history behind is that the Japanese took over many parts of Southeast Asia, forcing them to work in brutal conditions in order to get the resources they wanted. The Japanese soliders were extremely cruel, mercilessly killing hundreds of people.


Malaysia Independence 8/31/1963

Malaysia has a dark past on colonialism. From the British to Japan, they have been taking advantage of their resources and people. Throughout most of Malaysia's existance, they have been colonized by the British. To finally break out from colonialism, they were able to declare their independence on August 31!
